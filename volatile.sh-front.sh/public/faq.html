> FREQUENTLY_ASKED_QUESTIONS

Everything you need to know about volatile.sh

How secure is volatile.sh?

volatile.sh uses AES-256-GCM encryption, the same standard used by governments and militaries worldwide. Your secret is encrypted in your browser before it ever leaves your device. The decryption key is stored in the URL fragment (the part after #), which browsers never send to servers. This means we literally cannot read your secrets.

Can you see my secrets?

No. Zero-knowledge means we have zero knowledge of your data.

  • Your secret is encrypted client-side using Web Crypto API
  • The encryption key is in the URL fragment, never sent to our server
  • We only store encrypted ciphertext, which is useless without the key
  • Even if we were compelled by law, we could not decrypt your data

What happens if I lose the link?

Your secret is permanently lost. The link contains the decryption key. If you lose it, there is no recovery mechanism. This is intentional for security.

Why use this instead of email/Slack?

Email and chat platforms store your messages forever on their servers. They can be subpoenaed, breached, or mined for advertising. volatile.sh:

  • Stores nothing permanently (ephemeral RAM-only storage)
  • Destroys messages after reading (burn-after-reading)
  • Requires no account or personal information
  • Works with any recipient who has a web browser

Can I password-protect my secret?

The link itself acts as authentication. Anyone with the full link (including the fragment) can decrypt the secret. Treat the link like the secret itself. For additional security, share the link through a secure channel (encrypted messaging, in person, etc.).

What's the maximum secret size?

Approximately 1MB of text after encryption. This is about 500,000 words or roughly the length of a short novel. For most use cases (API keys, passwords, confidential messages), this is more than sufficient.

Do you log anything?

We log minimal operational data only:

  • Rate limit counters (hashed IP addresses, stored in RAM only)
  • Request metrics for capacity planning
  • We do not log: secret content, keys, or personal identifiers

Is this open source?

Yes! The source code is available on GitHub. You can review it, run your own instance, or contribute. Transparency is essential for security tools.

Can I self-host this?

Yes. volatile.sh is built on Cloudflare Workers and Durable Objects, which you can deploy to your own Cloudflare account. See the documentation for setup instructions.

What if volatile.sh shuts down?

All secrets have a maximum TTL of 7 days. Even if the service shut down immediately, any unread secrets would simply expire and be deleted. Your secrets are not dependent on our long-term existence.

How are secrets deleted?

Secrets are stored in Cloudflare Durable Objects with an automatic expiration alarm. When a secret is read, it is immediately deleted from the DO storage. Unread secrets are automatically deleted when their TTL expires. This is enforced at the platform level and cannot be bypassed.

Is the decryption key really never sent to the server?

Yes. This is a fundamental property of how URLs work. The fragment (everything after the #) is never included in HTTP requests. You can verify this yourself by opening your browser's developer tools and watching the network requests when creating or reading a secret—the fragment is nowhere to be found.

Security Reminders:

• Anyone with the link can read the secret—share it securely

• Browser extensions or screen recorders can capture your content

• Link previews in chat apps may send the URL to their servers (use caution)
